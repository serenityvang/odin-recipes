Espresso

Description

A rich, concentrated shot of coffee brewed under pressure, highlighting the bold flavors and aromas of freshly ground beans. The perfect foundation for lattes, cappuccinos, and other café favorites — or enjoyed on its own for a pure coffee experience.

Ingredients

  • Any type of coffee beans can be used:
    • Arabica -> smoother, sweeter, and often with fruity or floral notes
    • Robusta -> stronger, bouulder, higher caffine, often more bitter.
    • Blend -> common in cafes; balances smoothness with intensity
  • Use beans roasted for espresso(meidum to dark roast works best for classic flavors)
  • 7-9 g finely ground coffee beans (for a single shot)
  • Fresh filtered water

Instructions

  1. Grind the beans:
    • Grind freshly roasted beans to a fine consistency, like table salt
    • Espresso requires an espresso grinder for consistient, find grounds.
  2. Prepare the machine: (or aeropress or moka pot)
    • Preheat your espresso machine by running a blank shot (just water)
    • Lock in the portafilter once filled with grounds and tamp evenly with firm pressure
    • For the aeropress:
      • Insert filter, rinse with hot water.
      • Add coffee grounds to the AeroPress chamber
      • Pour hot water just above the grounds
      • Stir for ~10 seconds, then press down firmly
      • You will get a concentrated, espresso-like shot
  3. Brew:
    • Start the shot, aim for 25-30 seconds extraction time for a single shot (30ml)
    • The espresso shoulud flow like warm honey. Smooth and steady.
  4. Serve:
  5. Enjoy straight for a strong, rich taste.
  6. Or use as a base for lattes, cappucinos, macchiatos, and other cafe drinks
