Flat-White

Description

Originating from Australia and New Zealand, the flat white is a velvety espresso-based drink with a higher coffee-to-milk ratio than a latte. Itâ€™s made with rich espresso and steamed milk topped with a thin layer of foam. Smooth, creamy, and perfect for highlighting the natural flavors of the coffee.

Ingredients

  • 1 double shot espresso (60ml)
  • 1/3 or 1/2 cup steamed milk (about 150 ml)
  • Thin layer of foam (not think forth like a cappucino)

Instructions

  1. Brew the espresso
    • Pull a double shot of espresso into a small cup (traditionally 5-6 oz)
  2. Steam the milk
    • Use a steam wand to create microfoam: silky, velvety milk with very find bubbles
    • If no machine: heat milk gently, then froth using a frother, whisk, or French press until it's smooth and glossy (not too airy)
  3. Pour the milk
    • Hold the cup at an angle and pour the steamed milk slowly into the espresso
    • Aim for a thin, flossy layer of foam on top
  4. Serve
