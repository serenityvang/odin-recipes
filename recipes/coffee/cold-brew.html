Cold-Brew

Description

Cold brew is a refreshing, slow-brewed coffee made by steeping coarse coffee grounds in cold water for 12–18 hours. The result is a smooth, naturally sweet concentrate that’s less acidic than hot coffee. Served over ice, it’s bold yet easy to drink, perfect for warm days or as a base for creative café drinks.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup coarsely ground coffee beans (medium to dark roast works best)
  • 4 cupps cold, filtered water
  • Ice cubes
  • Optional: milk, cream, or flavored styrups (vanilla, caramel, hazelnut, etc.)

Instructions

  1. Combine
    • In a large jarm mix 1 cup coarlset grounded coffee with 4 cups cold water
    • Stir to ensure all grounds are fully saturated
  2. Steep
    • Cover and let sit at room temperature or in the fridge for 12-18 hours
    • Longer steep = stronger flavor
  3. Strain
    • Use a fine-mesh sieve, cheesecloth, or coffee filter to strain out the grounds.
    • You now have cold brew concentrate
  4. Serve
    • Pour over ice
    • Dilute with cold water or milk (usually 1 part concentrate: 1 part water/milk, adjust to taste)
    • Sweeten or flavor as desired
